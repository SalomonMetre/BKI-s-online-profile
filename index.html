About Me

Bridging theoretical chemistry and machine learning

I am a PhD candidate in Theoretical Chemistry at the University of Manchester, specializing in the design, implementation, and application of machine learning algorithms in computational chemistry. My current focus is on developing FFLUX, a physics-informed machine-learned force field that represents a significant advancement in molecular simulation methodologies.

Research Focus

Advancing computational chemistry through machine learning

FFLUX Force Field

Development of physics-informed machine-learned force fields that maintain quantum mechanical accuracy while achieving classical speed.

  • Quantum Theory of Atoms in Molecules (QTAIM) integration
  • Gaussian Process Regression optimization
  • Unprecedented temperature robustness

Machine Learning Algorithms

Design and implementation of novel ML algorithms specifically tailored for computational chemistry applications.

  • Hyperparameter optimization techniques
  • Transfer learning methodologies
  • Metaheuristic optimization approaches

Computational Chemistry

Application of advanced computational methods to understand molecular behavior and chemical phenomena.

  • Molecular dynamics simulations
  • Electronic structure calculations
  • Chemical property prediction
